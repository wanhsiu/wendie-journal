The article "The internet didn't kill counterculture- you just won't find it on Instagram" by Caroline Busta was a interesting read and it gave different points about what counterculter is today. Back then you would associate counterculter with a subcultures that is against the norm. For example, punks, goths, gabbers, etc. I feel like now it is a bit harder to be truly counterculter. The quote " To be truly counterculture in a time of tech hegemony, one has to, above all, betray the platform which may come in the form of betraying or divesting from your public online self." I feel like its saying in order to be consider counterculter these days you have to remove yourself from the internet not only through social media platforms but also your buying pattern and how you receive information. Another interesting point is how power is used on the web from politics to these large tech companies. I guess I grew up when the internet was just starting and what you did back then wasn't as big of an impact than it is today.

"The Good Room" by Frank Chimero really resonated with me, these are things that I already think about, my realtionship with the internet. I read more books in the past year than I have ever in my life. Mainly beacuase I had to force myself to get off the screen. From the pandemic to the clown show at the white house, there was way to many distractions. The constant scrolling and the speed at which we read or look at things, as he calls it "violent delivery". The graph that he shows comparing the apps with time and happiness. It's so truly sad that the apps that we should be using to better our mental health are ones that we spend less than 20 mins on. It seems like there is a ton of regret after hours of mindlessly scrolling through a social app. I am definately guilty of it and also have full of regrets :( lol.
