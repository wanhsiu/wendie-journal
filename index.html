I really enjoyey this reading and started reading more about the American Artist. I found it intersting his way of connecting antiblackness and white supremecy through his art in a form of technology. He mentions that in the 70s computer screens were black but now when you open up a document or go on a search engine the backgrounds are usually white. I think its clever that he's using this medium as a way to to show his ideological assertion of whiteness as neutral. I found his piece No State, 2018 really impactful. It was a installation with over a hundred shattered iphones placed on the floor. He contextualizes the black screen as a metaphore for Blackness: "Racial Slavery—which generated Blackness as a site of permanent extraction, gratuitous physical violence, and social death—provided the material and ideological basis for the United States. Unsurprisingly, Silicon Valley advances this constitutive anti-blackness through its technological products and processes". The shattered screens and broken device is a metaphor for Black people and how their bodies circulate in the possession of the priviledge hands. I found this very thought provoking. This makes me think about representation and what it means to be POC in this industry.
